Emacs becomes sensitive to the task at hand.
_Modes_ allows Emacs to the kind of editor you want for different tasks.
A buffer can be in only one _major mode_ at a time.
_Minor modes_ defines a particular aspect of Emacs's behavior and can be turned on and off within a major mode.

If you are good at Lisp programming, you can add your own modes.
Emacs is almost infinitely extensible.
